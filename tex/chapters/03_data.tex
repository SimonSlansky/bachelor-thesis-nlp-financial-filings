\section{Annual vs.\ Quarterly Filing Frequency}
\label{sec:annual_vs_quarterly}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A fundamental design decision for any study combining textual and financial
data from SEC filings is whether to use annual filings (Form~10-K, filed once
per fiscal year) or quarterly filings (Form~10-Q, filed for quarters Q1--Q3).
A quarterly approach would triple the number of observations but is only viable
if both financial and textual data are available and of sufficient quality.
We evaluate three dimensions: (i)~XBRL financial metric coverage,
(ii)~fourth-quarter data reliability, and (iii)~textual content availability.

\subsection{XBRL Financial Metric Coverage}

We compare the availability of nine financial metrics in 10-K (annual) and
10-Q (Q1--Q3) filings for a diagnostic sample of 30 large non-financial U.S.\
companies drawn from SEC EDGAR.  Table~\ref{tab:xbrl_coverage} reports the
share of firm-periods for which each metric can be extracted from XBRL.

\input{tables/xbrl_coverage}

Annual filings exhibit near-universal coverage across all metrics.
Quarterly filings generally show comparable coverage for balance-sheet items
and income-statement metrics, but operating cash flow is substantially less
available due to the SEC's practice of reporting year-to-date (rather than
standalone quarterly) cash-flow figures in 10-Q filings.

\subsection{Fourth-Quarter Reliability}
\label{sec:q4_reliability}

Even restricting quarterly data to Q1--Q3, a quarterly panel still requires
reliable Q4 figures if any year-over-year analysis is needed.  Ideally, Q4
would be computed as $\text{Annual} - \text{Q1} - \text{Q2} - \text{Q3}$, but
this requires all three quarterly values to exist.  In addition, some companies
file explicit Q4 period facts in XBRL, whose consistency with the implied value
is not guaranteed.

Table~\ref{tab:q4_reliability} summarises our diagnostic across three major
flow metrics.  For Net Income, Q1--Q3 facts are present in approximately
81\% of firm-years, but this drops to 80\% for Operating Income and only
7\% for Operating Cash Flow.  Among 244 observations where both explicit and
implied Q4 values exist across all tags, approximately 11\% exhibit material
mismatches exceeding 1\% of the annual figure.  Notable examples include
Amazon (FY2011, \$671M difference in net income) and Microsoft (FY2009--2012,
discrepancies exceeding \$1B), likely caused by restatements, amendments, or
differences in the scope of reported segments.

\input{tables/q4_reliability}

Operating Cash Flow is the most problematic: companies typically report
cumulative year-to-date cash flows in 10-Q filings rather than standalone
quarterly figures, making quarterly extraction nearly impossible for this metric.
Since accruals---a key variable for our H3 interaction term---depend directly on
operating cash flow, the quarterly approach would render this hypothesis
untestable.


\subsection{Textual Content Availability}
\label{sec:text_availability}

The most decisive factor for our frequency choice is the availability of
textual data.  Our NLP pipeline requires two sections: (i)~MD\&A
(Management's Discussion and Analysis)---Item~7 in 10-K, Part~I Item~2 in
10-Q---for computing $\Delta$Sentiment and TextSim, and (ii)~Risk Factors
(Item~1A in 10-K, Part~II Item~1A in 10-Q)---for computing $\Delta$Risk.

Table~\ref{tab:text_availability} reports the results of our filing-level
analysis of 15 companies across diverse sectors.

\input{tables/text_availability}

MD\&A is present in both filing types.  In 10-Q filings, the section is found
in approximately 93\% of filings and contains a meaningful discussion
(median $\sim$6{,}500 words), making it potentially viable for sentiment
analysis.  However, the 10-Q MD\&A covers only one quarter's results and
is therefore inherently shorter and narrower in scope than the annual Item~7.

\textbf{Risk Factors are the critical bottleneck.}  SEC Regulation S-K
Item~1A requires companies to update risk factors in 10-Q filings only when
there are ``material changes'' from those disclosed in the most recent 10-K.
In practice, our diagnostic shows that approximately 67\% of 10-Q filings
contain only a boilerplate reference (e.g., ``There have been no material
changes from the risk factors previously disclosed in our Annual Report on
Form~10-K''), and fully substantive risk-factor updates appear in fewer than
10\% of quarterly filings.  This renders the $\Delta$Risk variable---central
to H1 and H2---essentially impossible to compute from 10-Q filings.

Table~\ref{tab:text_per_company} in the appendix provides per-company detail,
confirming that the boilerplate pattern holds across all sectors: technology
(MSFT: 0/6 substantive), healthcare (JNJ, LLY: 0/6), energy (XOM, CVX:
mixed), consumer staples (KO, PG: 0/6), and retail (HD: 0/6).  Only
META~Platforms consistently reprints its full risk-factor section in every
10-Q, an outlier practice rather than the norm.


\subsection{Decision: Annual Panel}
\label{sec:frequency_decision}

Table~\ref{tab:annual_vs_quarterly} summarises the comparison across all
dimensions.

\input{tables/annual_vs_quarterly}

We adopt the \textbf{annual (10-K) frequency} for three reasons:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Textual feasibility:} The $\Delta$Risk variable, central
          to both H1 and H2, cannot be constructed from 10-Q filings.
          Dropping this variable would substantially weaken the research
          contribution.
    \item \textbf{Financial data quality:} Annual data avoids the Q4 gap,
          the year-to-date cash-flow problem, and the 11\% implied-Q4
          mismatch rate, yielding a cleaner panel.
    \item \textbf{Literature alignment:} Virtually all prior studies on
          textual analysis of SEC filings
          \parencite{loughran2011,li2008,blankespoor2020} use annual data,
          making our results directly comparable.
\end{enumerate}

The resulting panel comprises approximately 9{,}000 firm-years over
2009--2025, which provides ample statistical power for OLS estimation with
industry and year fixed effects.  The quarterly approach is documented as a
rejected alternative in this section for transparency and reproducibility.
